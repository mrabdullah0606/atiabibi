A Global Analysis of Renewable Energy and Sustainable Development: Role of Financial Development

This research evaluates the critical role of financial development in promoting renewable energy consumption and achieving sustainable development goals across a global panel of 113 countries from 1990 to 2021.

Methodology at a Glance

  • Data: Panel data of 113 countries (1990–2021)
  • Techniques: CIPS, Westerlund cointegration, FMOLS
  • Key Variables: Renewable energy, financial development index, SDI
  • Software: Stata

Key Learning Outcomes

  • Gained practical expertise in panel data econometrics
  • Managed cross-sectional dependence and non-stationarity
  • Identified long-run relationships through cointegration
  • Translated findings into policy-relevant insights

Does Social Accountability Matter for Education Sector? Perception of Young Female Students

This study explores female university students’ perceptions of social accountability in Pakistan’s education sector. It emphasizes the need to raise awareness to reduce social corruption and improve education sector performance.

Methodology at a Glance

  • Sample: 166 female university students
  • Data Collection: Online questionnaire
  • Analysis: Graphical illustration, Descriptive statistics
  • Tools: SPSS

Key Learning Outcomes

  • Designing and administering online questionnaires
  • Managing survey data effectively using SPSS
  • Developing skills in descriptive statistics & cross-tabulations
  • Analyzing socio-demographic differences in perceptions

Impact of Circular Economy on Climate Vulnerability: Role of Fiscal and Monetary Policy

This study analyzes the role of circular economy strategies—renewable energy, reuse, recycling, waste generation, and repair—in reducing climate vulnerability in EU countries (2005–2021).

Estimation Technique

  • Fixed Effects Panel Regression
  • Robust standard errors for heteroskedasticity
  • Panel unit root tests (Maddala-Wu and Pesaran CIPS)
  • Software: Stata

Key Learning Outcomes

  • Handling multi-country panel data over time
  • Construction of composite indices (CEI, FDI)
  • Expertise in fixed-effects regression models
  • Interpretation of results for real-world policy
